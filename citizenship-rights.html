Citizenship, Rights, and Duties

Description

This category outlines the rights and responsibilities of Indian citizens. It guarantees fundamental rights, sets expectations for the government's role in ensuring welfare (through directive principles), and lists the duties that citizens must uphold.

Part II: Citizenship

Specifies the qualifications for citizenship at the commencement of the Constitution and acquisition or termination of citizenship thereafter.

Part III: Fundamental Rights

Grants key rights to individuals, such as the right to equality, freedom, and protection from discrimination.

Part IV: Directive Principles of State Policy

Guidelines for the government to promote social, economic, and political justice, though not enforceable by law.

Part IV-A: Fundamental Duties

Lists the moral obligations citizens must follow, including respecting the Constitution and national symbols.
